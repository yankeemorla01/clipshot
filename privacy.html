Privacy Policy

The short version: nothing leaves your Mac. Ever.

Effective date: May 23, 2026
Last updated: May 23, 2026

TL;DR: ClipShot has no network code. It does not collect, transmit, share, or sell any data. Everything you do with ClipShot stays on your Mac.

What data ClipShot accesses

ClipShot reads the following data, locally on your Mac only:

  • Images on the clipboard. When you copy an image or take a screenshot to the clipboard, ClipShot detects it so it can save it to your history.
  • Screenshot files. ClipShot watches the folder you select for new screenshot files. Access is granted via a security-scoped bookmark you control through macOS's standard open panel.

What ClipShot does with that data

  • Saves screenshots and copied images locally in ~/Library/Containers/com.jcmorla.clipshot/Data/Library/Application Support/ClipShot/history/, organized by month and year.
  • Keeps a rolling history of your most recent captures, accessible from the menu bar.
  • Does not send anything to the internet. Ever. ClipShot contains no HTTP client, no URLSession calls, no telemetry SDK, no analytics framework.

What ClipShot does not do

  • ❌ Does not upload your screenshots to any server.
  • ❌ Does not send usage statistics, crash reports, or telemetry.
  • ❌ Does not sync to iCloud, Dropbox, or any cloud service by default.
  • ❌ Has no user accounts, logins, or authentication.
  • ❌ Does not share data with third parties — the developer has no access to your information.
  • ❌ Does not use cookies, advertising IDs, or fingerprinting.

Logs

ClipShot writes a technical log file to ~/Library/Containers/com.jcmorla.clipshot/Data/Library/Logs/ClipShot.log (rotated at 1 MB, permissions 0600 — only your user can read it). It contains only non-PII technical events like timestamps of configuration changes, I/O errors, and internal warnings. It never contains image content or paths that would reveal user activity.

Deleting your data

You can clear your history at any time from the menu: Clear history…, with options to delete everything or only captures older than 30 days. Uninstalling the app removes its data automatically when you delete the container at ~/Library/Containers/com.jcmorla.clipshot/.

System permissions

ClipShot may prompt for macOS permissions to access folders such as Desktop, Downloads, Documents, or Pictures — only if you configured macOS to save screenshots there. These permissions are managed by macOS, not the app, and can be granted or revoked at any time in System Settings → Privacy & Security.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated in future versions of ClipShot. The "Last updated" date above reflects the most recent change. Material changes will be communicated in the release notes for the corresponding version.
